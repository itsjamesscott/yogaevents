What Makes Yoga Inclusive in Berlin?

1. Multilevel Classes in English & German

Berlin’s inclusive studios offer multilevel group practices where:

  • Modifications and props are used for all abilities

  • Teachers provide options for beginners, intermediate, and advanced students in the same session

  • Classes are often available in both English and German to support international residents and visitors

2. Accessible Yoga: Chair, Restorative & Gentle Flow

Specially designed sessions cater to:

  • Seniors seeking gentle movement and relaxation

  • Individuals recovering from injuries or living with disabilities

  • Those new to yoga and wanting a slower, more mindful approach

Chair Yoga, Restorative Yoga, and Yin Yoga are commonly found throughout the city’s leading studios, often with certified teachers in adaptive movement.

3. All Bodies, All Backgrounds: Diversity and Belonging

Inclusive yoga classes in Berlin openly welcome:

  • People of all shapes, sizes, and physical conditions

  • LGBTQIA+ community members and allies

  • Non-binary and gender-diverse individuals

  • Beginners who may otherwise feel intimidated joining a group class

Studios create safe, respectful spaces, actively encouraging dialogue and feedback to improve access and comfort.

4. Trauma-Informed & Mindful Practices

Some Berlin teachers specialise in trauma-sensitive yoga, making practices emotionally safe and supportive. These classes:

  • Focus on breath awareness and self-regulation

  • Avoid physical adjustments unless requested

  • Foster a sense of safety and choice throughout the session

5. Outdoor & Community Yoga Events

Berlin offers free or donation-based yoga in parks, plazas, and community centers, lowering cost barriers and encouraging more people to join in mindful movement.
